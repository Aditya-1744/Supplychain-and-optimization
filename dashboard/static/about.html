System Architecture & Methodology

A 3rd-year Computer Science capstone project demonstrating applied machine learning in supply chain logistics. This platform integrates historical sales data with advanced forecasting models to automate replenishment planning and optimize inventory safety stock levels, reducing stockouts and minimising holding costs across a multi-echelon distribution network of 5 warehouses and 50 SKUs.

Forecasting Engine

Fourier Seasonal Regression + GBM

Fourier regression (via scikit-learn Ridge) decomposes demand into trend, weekly, and annual seasonality components. Gradient Boosting handles erratic Z-class SKUs. A production router automatically selects the appropriate model per SKU based on its ABC/XYZ classification and demand coefficient of variation.

Routing Rules
  • intermittent → Croston / Moving-Average baseline
  • smooth + X/Y → Fourier Seasonal Model
  • smooth + Z → GBM (disagreement flag)
account_tree

Optimization Engine

Multi-Echelon (s, S) Policy

Safety stock computed via the formula SS = Z × σ × √L. A NumPy discrete-event simulation engine (des_engine.py) runs (s, S) policy trajectories over 90-day holdout windows. Multi-echelon pooling math quantifies the coordination saving from centralised stock management versus decentralised per-store policies.
